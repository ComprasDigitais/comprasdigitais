        Rotina recomendada de uso

        A rotina diária segue três blocos:

        • Monitoramento matinal: analise os sinais gerados nas 08:00‑10:00 GMT.

        Essa sequência reduz a exposição a ruído e aumenta a taxa de acerto.

        Erros comuns e como evitá‑los

        Um deslizamento frequente é confiar em um único indicador. Quando isso acontece, a taxa de falsos positivos sobe rapidamente. Para contornar, implemente a regra “3‑de‑5”: só execute trade se pelo menos três dos cinco filtros gerarem sinal simultaneamente. Outro deslize é não atualizar os parâmetros após mudanças de volatilidade; ajuste-os mensalmente usando o optimizer do MetaEditor.

        Newbies chasing a “set‑and‑forget” Holy Grail will choke. The checklist is unforgiving: you need comfort with MQL5’s object‑oriented syntax, a baseline of indicator knowledge, and the patience to tweak filters for each asset class. If you expect a black‑box plug‑and‑play, skip this.

        Practical Limits

        Performance plateaus after roughly 20 concurrent checks; adding more indicators yields diminishing returns and can trigger latency spikes on lower‑spec VPSes. Memory usage climbs linearly with each filter, so a 2 GB RAM host caps out around 5‑6 complex custom studies. Runtime jitter is highest during news‑driven volatility—plan trades around scheduled releases if possible.

        Contextual FAQ

        Q: Is coding experience mandatory?
        I think you need at least a basic grasp of MQL5 classes. The documentation walks you through; however, copying without understanding will crash your EA.

        Q: Can I use it for non‑Forex symbols?
        Yes, but you must re‑calibrate filters for futures, stocks, or cryptos. The underlying logic is asset‑agnostic.

        Q: What if my broker’s spreads are wide?
        Filters that require tight spreads will constantly re‑reject signals, draining CPU. Adjust the spread threshold in Settings or switch to a tighter broker.
